Welcome to Popmundo

Popmundo is an online role-playing game and community where the players strive for fame in a virtual music industry.

You start the game as a complete nobody with nothing but your hopes and dreams. You can practice your music, play a few gigs, and begin to build a fanbase. Play your cards right and you'll soon be touring the world, cutting chart-topping records, and leaving a wake of tantalizing scandals behind you!

The game is played in real time and the game keeps on going even when you're not logged in. Success in the game is determined by careful strategy and good timing, not by constant online presence. It's perfectly possible to be successful in the game by logging in a couple of times a week to check up on things.

Popmundo is free of charge and no downloads are necessary to play it. If you can read this you're good to go.

This Week in Real Life #32
4/18/2019

Spring is finally here and the Easter bunny is about to make its annual appearance any day now. It's been a somewhat slow week with one dev off doing crazy stuff on a snow covered mountain with a piece of wood strapped to his feet. And as tradition has it: when the cat's away the mice will play!

Read more ¬ª

Despite the extra play time we did manage to get some work done. For example it's no longer necessary for band members to accept invitations to participate in their own music videos, instead they'll be automatically added as participants when invited. And the next time you get your weekly dose of experience points the diary event will tell you how many points you have in total, and not only how many points you got. We also deleted another 100 million rows of data which was hard to reach.

Parties are still in beta. A huge thank you to everyone who's been helping out by throwing soirees the last few weeks! We're currently ironing out some elusive bugs, and adding layer upon layer of debugging code to try to catch them. By next week we're hoping to start adding new features, events and attractions instead.

Enjoy the rest of the week people!

This Week in Real Life #31
4/11/2019

This will be a short twirl. We've already given our translators enough work for one week. But... in case anyone was wondering, winter came back to bite us in the butt one last time.

Read more ¬ª

It's been a busy week with the public beta of the party system and the launch of skateboards, hoverboards, and stormswingers. If you missed any of this please check out the community news on this page for more information. The implementation of the Stormswinger accidentally caused the toaster-obsessed robot on the moon to malfunction for a while, but other than that, it has been pretty smooth sailing so far. It's always a nice surprise when a new piece of code causes a butterfly effect to knock out something seemingly unrelated elsewhere in the game.

We also made it possible for artists to have band members direct their own music videos this week. And we sent the database to the shrink to get rid of two hundred million rows of repressed memories. That was interesting. The database is still undergoing treatment to get rid of some more traumatic data but we expect it to feel better real soon.

Enjoy the rest of the week people!
